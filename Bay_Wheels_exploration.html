Bay Wheels Usage Patterns 2019¶

by Amir Ragab¶

Bay Wheels(formerly known as Ford GoBike) is a regional public bicycle sharing system in the San Francisco Bay Area, California. Beginning operation in August 2013 as Bay Area Bike Share, the Ford GoBike system currently has over 2,600 bicycles in 262 stations across San Francisco, East Bay and San Jose. On June 28, 2017, the system officially launched as Ford GoBike in a partnership with Ford Motor Company.

Ford GoBike, like other bike share systems, consists of a fleet of specially designed, sturdy and durable bikes that are locked into a network of docking stations throughout the city. The bikes can be unlocked from one station and returned to any other station in the system, making them ideal for one-way trips. The bikes are available for use 24 hours/day, 7 days/week, 365 days/year and riders have access to all bikes in the network when they become a member or purchase a pass.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The final dataset contains the combined data of each of the 12 months. It contains more than 2,200,000 individual records and 19 variables.

The variables include the start and end of the trip, the start and end stations ids and names, the user type, and the stations' latitudes and longititude.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

I am mostly interested in exploring the times in the year bikes are used the most, along with the user type that uses them the most, along with the duration of the trips.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

The most helpful features would be the trips' start date/time, the month, and the duration will help with understanding how long a trip usually takes and when does it begin and end.
